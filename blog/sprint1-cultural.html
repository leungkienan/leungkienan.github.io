Describing personal values

Looking into the core values that I resonate with the most are health, personal fulfillment and friendship with many other values intertwined with these listed. I find my health to be an area I maintain the most, whether it is mentally or physically, I strive to keep myself in a state of happiness and to keep myself looking forward. I also take into consideration other people’s health, so I try to keep in touch with friends to make sure they’re doing well.

Ethical Dilemma

One time that called my values into action was when one of my mates tried to drive home after a long night of drinking. I quickly noticed that they had too much to drink and were in no condition to go home, so I would calmly explain to them that not only their health is at risk, but the lives of other people. I wanted to make sure that no one was being endangered from a situation that could be easily avoided and I’m glad that I convinced them to not get in their car.

Cultural Influence

My parents share a Chinese and New Zealand background, so my identity and values are influenced from those cultures. A lot of the things I enjoy stem from my ethnicity, but the values I’ve listed all come from the ideas that my parents and people around me have entrenched within me.

Strengths and Limitations with my learning

Looking at my strengths in regards to learning, I tend to learn things quicker from trying them out or from visual aid. I do well at tasks that provide a template or have clear instructions for how a task is to be completed, rather than having it explained verbally. I’d say I’m a much stronger visual learner than an auditory learner. I also get along with people well, so with team exercises, I feel comfortable working around strangers to learn more about their ideas and their perspectives.

What strengths will help my learning journey

I think that my strengths above will provide me great opportunities to excel at learning at EDA. I think my confidence with teamwork will help me gel well with others in my cohort and make things more enjoyable to get through. I think if I were to hit a roadblock in my learning, I feel like I would be able to ask another peer for help and get through the issue together.

Example of teamwork not going the best way

Back at university, I did many team-oriented projects, but one that stood out the most was my auditing group presentation. A lot of the questions we were tasked involved a lot of specific accounting law to figure out the correct answer. We were all new to auditing, so we spent a lot of time together understanding different pieces of law and applying it to the cases we had to do. A lot of arguing over what should be the best course of action, but we always came to a conclusion that we were satisfied with. We would break down each others perspectives to understand how we went through the questions individually and then piece together a cohesive answer. Our group was successful in presenting and our tutor had issues with our results and was happy about our project. I think this experience was the most cohesive and memorable one for me as it showed me that a group can have disputes, but you can be capable of coming up with the best results if others are open to understanding one another.
