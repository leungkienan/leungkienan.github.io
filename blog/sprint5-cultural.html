Reflections

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

The three things I learnt about myself are the following:

I enjoy learning new things, I may struggle through a tough issue, but I will put in the effort to get through and solve it.

I found myself to be quite harsh on my own health when I am absorbed with learning. When I get stuck into something I enjoy, I find it hard to stop to take breaks and refresh myself.

I am more aware of my emotions as I go through my day, so I’ve been trying to reward myself more for the accomplishments I do to motivate myself.

I have found that I get a (positive) ego boost when I feel a sense of accomplishment as I believe I’m learning more as I get through these sprints.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

The role of values in programming play into how one may go about solving an issue.

Empathy helps others understand each other’s interpretations of homogenous issues. Being exposed to others who are stuck in similar situations and sharing that experience connects us and lets us work together to tackle that problem.

Self-awareness makes us more aware about your reactions to different situations. I think that those who are self-aware have a greater understanding and smarter approach towards problems.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I was surprised by how much influence these aspects of core learning had on my foundations experience. I became more mindful of how I approached difficult situations and have a stronger growth mindset doing so.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

It was difficult for me to lay out my learning plan to a state that I deemed acceptable. I found it a bit difficult sticking to a plan with self-made boundaries.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

I think the core learning allows us to get a better understanding of who we are and grab a better insight of what and why we do things. I found core learning to be a unique experience, as college and university never exposed me to think more critically about my emotions and mindset.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

I feel like most aspects of the core learning opened my eyes to a new approach of learning. I think that being exposed to different styles of learning can be crucial to understanding different people’s perspectives and ideas. Although, I feel my hard skills could have been improved on with more time, but that lack of knowledge came down to how much time I spent working on these skills


Preparing for Bootcamp

What is your long-term goal and/or career pathway?

I have spent a bit of time studying Accounting and Finance at University, so I want to take this knowledge with me to a web development role in a tech-commerce industry. I want to utilise programming with a twist of commerce as I’ve enjoyed learning both subjects.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think my resilience to keep focus and fight through difficult tasks can be both a strength and limitation for me. I think it will come down to how I learn from the people around me and my desire to learn.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think my biggest non-technical challenge will just be getting comfortable talking to new people. I’ve recognised that it takes me a bit of time for me to connect and open up with people, but I try my best to get things done when necessary

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I think I want to improve on my confidence in front of strangers. I don’t put myself into those types of situations often, so I think EDA will provide me a great opportunity to work on this at bootcamp

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I think I expect myself to maintain at a pace on par with my cohort. I want to ensure that I’m learning the content thoroughly and I’m spending my time effectively. I don’t want to be the one dragging the team behind and will put in the effort to maintain that pace.
